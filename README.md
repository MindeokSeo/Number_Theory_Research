# Undergraduate Research in Number Theory

## 📌 Overview
[cite_start]This repository documents my undergraduate research in number theory at Korea University, conducted under the advisement of Prof. Dae Jun Kim[cite: 12]. [cite_start]The research is structured into two main phases: a Directed Reading Program (DRP) focusing on elementary number theory [cite: 17][cite_start], and an ongoing computational investigation into additive number theory and quadratic forms[cite: 18].

## 📅 Project Timeline & Activities

### Phase 1: Directed Reading Program (Sep 2025 - Dec 2025)
* [cite_start]**Objective**: Establish a strong theoretical foundation in elementary number theory and conduct an in-depth literature review[cite: 17].
* **Primary Textbook**: *Elementary Number Theory and Its Applications* (6th Edition) by Bart Goddard and Kenneth H. Rosen.
* **Key Literature**: *On Euler's Totient Function* by D. H. Lehmer.
* [cite_start]**Focus**: Understanding the Lehmer property [cite: 17] and developing the ability to critically read and synthesize foundational mathematical papers.

### Phase 2: Additive Number Theory & Quadratic Forms (Jan 2026 - Present)
* [cite_start]**Objective**: Investigate additive number theory, specifically focusing on the universality of weighted sums of generalized polygonal numbers[cite: 18].
* **Key Literature**: 
  * [cite_start]*Weighted sums of generalized polygonal numbers with coefficients 1 or 2* by Daejun Kim[cite: 45, 47].
  * [cite_start]*A Short Proof of Cauchy's Polygonal Number Theorem* by Melvyn B. Nathanson[cite: 389, 405].
* **Computational Implementation**:
  * [cite_start]Developed and implemented a Python algorithm utilizing escalator trees to computationally discover and verify universal coefficient tuples for generalized 9-gonal numbers[cite: 19].
  * Applied the mathematical concept of a **"truant"** (the smallest positive integer not represented by a given linear combination) to systematically construct and prune branches within the escalator trees.
  * Verified candidates to determine if they successfully generate all natural numbers up to a specified limit.

## 📂 Repository Structure
* [cite_start]`src/`: Python source code containing the escalator tree algorithm, truant verification logic, and polygonal number generators[cite: 19].
* [cite_start]`docs/`: Summaries, literature review notes on the Lehmer property [cite: 17][cite_start], Cauchy's polygonal number theorem [cite: 422][cite_start], and generalized polygonal numbers[cite: 68].
